SEO Optimzation Plan - TIPS
Based on browsing discussions from copywriters and SEO professionals on Reddit (particularly in subreddits like r/SEO, r/Copywriting, and r/marketing), there is a very strong consensus on this exact struggle.

Here is what the professionals on Reddit have to say about balancing heavy SEO with punchy, attention-grabbing copy:

1. "Write for Humans First, Bots Second" (The Golden Rule)
The most repeated advice on Reddit is that if you sacrifice your brand voice to please an algorithm, you will lose the customer anyway.

The "Bounce" problem: If a user clicks on an SEO-optimized link but the website reads like a robot wrote it, they hit the "back" button immediately. Google tracks this "bounce rate." If your bounce rate is high, Google penalizes your ranking anyway. Unnatural SEO copy literally defeats its own purpose.
The "Read it Out Loud" Test: Redditors constantly advise: “Read your copy out loud. If you wouldn’t say it to a client's face without feeling awkward, delete it.”
2. Stop Chasing "Exact Match" Keywords
A few years ago, you had to write exactly "Best Office Based Anesthesia Record App" over and over. Reddit SEOs emphasize that Google’s NLP (Natural Language Processing) is much smarter now.

You don't need to force clunky phrases.
If you write about "Anesthesia," "Sedation," "Charting," and "Compliance" naturally throughout the page, Google understands what the app does without you having to write a clunky, run-on sentence in your main headline.
3. Separation of Page Purpose
Redditors frequently point out that not every page needs to be an SEO workhorse.

Conversion Pages (Homepage): The job of the homepage is to capture attention and convert. Keep the copy punchy, emotional, and highly aesthetic here.
Traffic Pages (Blogs/Articles/Guides): The job of these pages is to bring in organic search traffic. This is where you put your heavy SEO text, long FAQs, and detailed explanations.
4. How They Actually Inject the Text (The Tactics)
When a client demands heavy SEO text but wants to keep the site looking beautiful, Reddit copywriters use a few specific visual tricks:

The "Expandable" Content: They use sleek Accordions or "Read More" dropdowns. The page looks minimal, but if Google crawls it (or a human clicks it), the heavy SEO text expands.
The "Fat Footer": They place beautiful, minimalist copy on the top 80% of the website. Then, right above the footer, they include a clean, muted text block (often an FAQ) that houses the heavier keyword content.
Secondary Elements: They put the punchy copy in the massive <h1> hero font, and slip the SEO keywords into smaller sub-text, caption text under images, or bulleted feature lists further down the page where it feels natural.
